Intuitive Music Theory

Part Two: What is it all for?

Recap

  • Understanding pitch is an important part of understanding music
  • We can break pitch up into little units, and learn to hear them
  • Those little units can be used to build more interesting musical things

What can we use them for?

Scales

  • A sequence of notes, often numbering seven.
  • Many songs contain notes from just one scale
  • Given a starting note, build a scale by adding semitones/tones,
    collecting each new note along the way.

Chords

  • Three or more notes, often played at the same time
  • May be built out of notes from a scale
  • Combine two semitones/tones to achieve bigger gaps

Closing Remarks

It's all relative:

  • One major scale is as good as another
  • What is true for one major scale is true for any major scale
  • One minor scale is as good as another
  • What is true for one minor scale is true for any minor scale
  • One major chord is as good as another
  • What is true for one major chord is true for any major chord
  • One minor chord is as good as another
  • What is true for one minor chord is true for any minor chord

Closing Remarks

  • Ear training is the most valuable music theory
  • Music theory is a language, not a rule book
  • This is just the beginning.
